Caliber (Intel Caliber) - Full Chip Timing Signoff Overview
============================================================
Last updated: 2026-03-30

WHAT IS CALIBER?
  Caliber is Intel's full-chip timing signoff checker.
  It runs AFTER partition-level STA (PrimeTime) and checks physical/electrical
  violations at the FC (Full Chip) level: capacitance, slopes, pulse widths,
  clock integrity, latch margins, and more.

  Caliber violations are DIFFERENT from STA timing violations:
  - STA (PrimeTime): setup/hold slack on paths (functional timing)
  - Caliber: physical/electrical rule checks (signal quality, clock health)
  Both must be clean for signoff.

HOW TO RUN CALIBER:
  # Partition-level extract-fc:
  intel_caliber -B $block -F intel_caliber --extract_parent_fc core_client --extract_parent_tag GFCN2CLIENTA0LATEST

  # PO must provide the tag to populate from in order to get reports from archive

CORNERS CHECKED:
  func.max_high.T_85.typical  (setup, fast process, high voltage)
  func.max_nom.T_85.typical   (setup, fast process, nominal voltage)
  func.max_low.T_85.typical   (setup, fast process, low voltage)

VIOLATION SEVERITY:
  "must"        -- Must be fixed for signoff
  "recommended" -- Should be fixed, not blocking
  "info"        -- Informational only

VIOLATION OWNERS:
  PO      -- Partition Owner
  PO_PV   -- Partition Owner / Physical Verification (most common)
  PO_SYN  -- Partition Owner / Synthesis
  NA      -- Not assigned / infrastructure

NOTE ON IFC:
  Caliber violations are NOT the same as IFC timing violations.
  - Most caliber violations are internal to a partition (not cross-partition).
  - The "partitions" column in .rpt files shows where the net is routed.
  - "core_client" means routed at FC level but may still be internal.
  - True cross-partition: partitions column contains ";" (multiple partitions).
  - The "is_interface" column (in SlowSlope) marks IFC nets explicitly.
