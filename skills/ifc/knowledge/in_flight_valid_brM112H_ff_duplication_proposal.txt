========================================
1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
========================================

  Problem:
  7-bit bus "in_flight_valid_brM112H[0][0:6]" crossing par_msid → par_fe
  is FAILING on ALL 7 bits (slack -33 to -61 ps). Systematic bus-level
  failure caused by a 5-stage buffer chain (~83 ps) driving signal 383 μm
  from source FF to partition boundary.

  Solution:
  Duplicate 7 source FFs near the par_msid boundary. Eliminates the
  buffer chain entirely. Expected gain: ~75 ps → closes ALL 7 bits.

  CONCLUSION: Both neighbor stages have margin. Retiming is viable
  but FF duplication is simpler and more effective for this case.

========================================
13. ALTERNATIVE FIXES CONSIDERED (AND WHY REJECTED)
========================================

  Technique 1 — Cell Upsizing:
    Replace SR2BFY buffers with stronger cells.
    Expected gain: ~15-20 ps. NOT enough to close -61 ps.
    REJECTED as standalone fix.

  Technique 2 — Remove SR2BFY Cells:
    Replace scan-repair-friendly buffers with standard buffers.
    Expected gain: ~20-30 ps. May not close worst bits.
    COULD BE COMBINED with other techniques.

  Technique 6 — Retiming:
    Borrow time from neighbor stages.
    Both neighbors have margin, but -61 ps is too much to borrow
    without risking failures on neighbor paths.
    REJECTED as standalone fix.

  Technique 8 — Port Spec Rebudget:
    Change timing budget at partition boundary.
    Possible but pushes problem to par_fe side which already has
    deep logic (119 ps). May create new failures.
    NOT RECOMMENDED.

  Technique 9 — Placement Optimization:
    Move cells closer. Limited gain since distance is inherent
    to partition floorplan.
    COULD HELP but won't solve 61 ps gap alone.

  ▶ TECHNIQUE 5 (FF DUPLICATION) was selected because:
    - Provides largest gain (~75 ps) — closes ALL bits with margin
    - Minimal area/power cost
    - Clean implementation (RTL + constraint)
    - Only risk is 1-cycle latency — needs architecture review
