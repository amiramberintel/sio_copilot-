estimate_eco GOTCHAS
====================

1. GBA vs PBA MISMATCH
   estimate_eco uses GBA slack internally.
   The absolute slack values are NOT your PBA slack.
   Only the DELTA (improvement) transfers to PBA results.
   NEVER report GBA numbers from estimate_eco as actual slack.

2. *current* = BEST means NO ECO HELPS
   If the first row shows *current* as best option,
   this cell is already optimal. Move on to next cell.

3. BUFFER INSERTION IS OPTIMISTIC
   estimate_eco assumes ideal buffer placement.
   Real gains are typically 60-80% of estimated due to routing.

4. CROSS-PARTITION LIMITATION
   You can only ECO cells in YOUR partition.
   If the bottleneck cell is in another partition,
   estimate_eco results are informational only.

5. DULVT ALREADY OPTIMAL
   Most GFC Client A0 critical paths are already DULVT.
   VT swap options may show no improvement.
   Try insert_buffer on long nets instead.

6. CHECK BOTH RISE AND FALL
   Worst transition may differ. Always check both:
   estimate_eco ... -rise ...
   estimate_eco ... -fall ...

7. DONT_USE CELLS IN RESULTS
   estimate_eco may suggest cells on the dont_use list!
   Always cross-check against skills/cell_library/knowledge/illegal_cells_dont_use.txt
   before recommending an ECO.

8. CORNER MISMATCH
   Always run estimate_eco on the SAME corner as your timing analysis.
   Setup: func.max_high (or whichever is limiting)
   Hold:  func.min_low / func.min_fast (with -min flag)
